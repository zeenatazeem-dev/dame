Fish meal, sometimes spelt fishmeal, is a commercial product made from whole wild-caught fish, bycatch, and fish by-products to feed farm animals, e.g., pigs, poultry, and farmed fish.

The main steps of the process are cooking for coagulation of the protein thereby liberating bound water and oil, separation by pressing of the coagulate yielding a solid phase (presscake) containing 60-80% of the oil-free dry matter (protein, bones) and oil, and a liquid phase (press liquor) containing water and the rest of the solids (oil, dissolved and suspended protein, vitamins and minerals).

The three major sources of fish meal are: fish stocks harvested specifically for this purpose: small, bony and oily fish such as anchovy, horse mackerel, menhaden, capelin, sandeel, blue whiting, herring, pollack… by-catches from other fisheries

The digestible energy values in terms of Kcal/g of the ingredients are 3.68, 3.22, 4.38 and 4.44 Kcal/g for fish meal, dextrin, fish oil and soybean oil respectively.

Fish meal generally contains 60% to 72% protein, 5% to 12% fats and 10% to 20% ash. It also contains high amount of fatty acids among them the most common is omega-3 fatty acid. Fish meal directly influences the efficiency of feed having amino acid, phosphorus and fatty acid.

Fish meal of good quality normally contains between 60% and 72% crude protein by weight. From a nutritional standpoint, FM is one of the preferred animal protein supplements in the diets of farm animals. A typical inclusion rate of FM in terrestrial livestock diets is usually 5% or less on a dry matter basis.
